About

Lasagna is both a wide, flat pasta shape and a classic Italian baked casserole dish. The dish is assembled by stacking layers of pasta alternating with fillings like savory meat or tomato sauce, béchamel sauce, and various cheeses, then baked until bubbly.

  • Aromatics
  • Tomato Base
  • Seasonings
  • Soft Cheeses
  • Binder

Steps

  1. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and brown the ground beef and Italian sausage until crumbly.
  2. Drain any excess grease from the pan.
  3. Stir in the diced onion and minced garlic, cooking for 3 to 4 minutes until softened.
  4. Pour in the marinara sauce and tomato paste.
  5. Add the Italian seasoning, then lower the heat and let the sauce simmer for 15 minutes.
  6. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the lasagna noodles for 8 to 10 minutes until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  7. In a medium bowl, thoroughly mix the ricotta cheese, beaten egg, Parmesan cheese, and fresh parsley together.
  8. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  9. Layer 1: Spread 1 cup of meat sauce on the bottom of the dish. Top with 3 or 4 lasagna noodles.
  10. Layer 2: Spread 1/3 of the ricotta mixture over the noodles, followed by a sprinkle of mozzarella cheese and another layer of meat sauce.
  11. Repeat: Add noodles, ricotta, mozzarella, and meat sauce two more times until you reach the final layer of noodles.
  12. Top Layer: Finish with the remaining meat sauce and a generous layer of shredded mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  13. Cover the baking dish tightly with aluminum foil. Tip: Spray the inside of the foil with cooking spray so the cheese doesn't stick.
  14. Bake in the preheated oven for 30 minutes.
  15. Remove the foil and bake uncovered for an additional 15 minutes until the cheese is golden and bubbling.
  16. Remove from the oven and let the lasagna rest for 15 minutes before slicing so it holds its shape.
