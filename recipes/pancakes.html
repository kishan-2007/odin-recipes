About

Pancakes are flat, round cakes prepared from a starch-based batter containing eggs, milk, and butter. Cooked on a hot griddle or frying pan, they expand during the cooking process to yield a light, fluffy texture ideal for breakfast menus.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Sugar
  • Baking Powder
  • Salt
  • Milk
  • Melted Butter
  • Beaten Egg
  • Maple Syrup

Steps

  1. Heat a large flat griddle or non-stick frying pan over medium-high heat until hot.
  2. Wipe away any dust or moisture from the surface of the pan.
  3. Whisk the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt together in a large mixing bowl.
  4. Pour the milk, melted butter, and beaten egg into a separate container.
  5. Stir the liquid ingredients into the dry ingredients slowly until the mixture forms a thick batter.
  6. Allow the pancake batter to rest for 2 minutes to activate the leavening agents.
  7. Lightly grease the heated griddle surface with a small amount of additional butter or oil.
  8. Preheat your cooking surface completely before pouring the first batch of batter.
  9. Layer 1: Pour exactly 1/4 cup of batter onto the hot surface to form a circle.
  10. Layer 2: Monitor the surface as bubbles begin to rise and break across the top face.
  11. Repeat this placement for as many individual pancakes as your pan surface can comfortably hold.
  12. Top Layer: Wait until the edges look dry and set before attempting to flip the pancake.
  13. Slide a flat spatula completely underneath the pancake structure to lift it cleanly.
  14. Bake or sear the opposite side for 1 to 2 minutes until it mirrors the golden color.
  15. Remove the finished pancakes from the hot surface and stack them on a plate.
  16. Drizzle with maple syrup and serve immediately while the center remains warm and soft.
